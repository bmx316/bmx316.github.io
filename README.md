# Studer Equipment Liquidation Playbook

A simple, printable, step-by-step playbook for one machinist to liquidate a warehouse of Studer cylindrical grinders, CNC machines, parts, and supplies — without becoming a salesperson.

## Files

- **[`PRD.md`](./PRD.md)** — the main playbook. Start here. The "do this on Monday" list is in Section 11.
- **[`buyer-call-list.md`](./buyer-call-list.md)** — every dealer, liquidator, auction house, and parts buyer to email in week 1, with status tracking.
- **[`inventory-template.csv`](./inventory-template.csv)** — the spreadsheet to fill out, one row per machine / part / bin.
- **[`listing-copy-template.md`](./listing-copy-template.md)** — copy/paste templates for MachineTools.com, Surplus Record, eBay, etc.
- **[`bill-of-sale-template.md`](./bill-of-sale-template.md)** — one-page bill of sale for every transaction.
- **[`scam-checklist.md`](./scam-checklist.md)** — red flags. Print and keep next to the phone.

## The whole thing in one paragraph

Inventory the warehouse in Week 1 (one spreadsheet, photos, short videos). Email the Big List to the 6 liquidators, 7 auction houses, and 9 Studer specialists in `buyer-call-list.md` in Week 2. Compare offers in Week 3 and pick one of three paths: a **one-call buyout** (fastest, ~30% of value), an **auction** (recommended, ~60% of value, 8–14 weeks), or a **DIY listing campaign** for only the 2–5 most valuable machines (highest dollar, hardest work). The full reasoning, scripts, templates, and pricing guides are in `PRD.md`.
