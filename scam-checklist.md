# Scam & Red-Flag Checklist

Print this. Tape it next to the phone. Read it every time before you reply to a buyer.

If you see **any one** of these, walk away. There are real buyers — you don't need to take the risky one.

---

## In the email / first contact

- [ ] **Generic greeting** ("Dear Sir/Madam," "Hello Seller") with no reference to the specific machine or your name → **scam**
- [ ] **Bad English mixed with formal legal-sounding phrases** ("Kindly do the needful," "Your soonest reply is most appreciated") → **scam**
- [ ] **Wants to buy without asking a single technical question** → **scam**
- [ ] **Free email domain (gmail/yahoo/hotmail) claiming to be a multinational buyer** → **scam**
- [ ] **Says they're a "purchasing agent" or "buying for a client overseas"** and won't name the client → **scam**

## In the offer

- [ ] **Offers more than you asked** — they always have a "reason" (shipper fees, customs broker) that requires you to wire money back → **always a scam**
- [ ] **Wants to send a cashier's check or "certified" check** → **scam.** Counterfeits clear initially and bounce 2–4 weeks later, after pickup.
- [ ] **Wants to use Western Union, MoneyGram, gift cards, crypto** → **scam**
- [ ] **Insists on using "their" escrow service** — almost always a fake escrow site → **scam**
- [ ] **Pushes you to act fast** ("My truck is in your area tomorrow only") → **scam pressure tactic**
- [ ] **Refuses to send a Certificate of Insurance for their rigger** → **scam or unprofessional, walk away**

## In the logistics

- [ ] **Wants to pick up before payment clears** → **always no**
- [ ] **"My freight forwarder will collect, just give them the items"** without payment in your account → **scam**
- [ ] **Driver shows up with no name on the truck, no paperwork** → **stop the load, call the buyer**

## What a REAL buyer looks like

- ✅ Asks specific, technical questions about the machine ("hours on the spindle," "is the Granit control intact," "what brand is the wheel dresser," "can you send a picture of the way scraping")
- ✅ Wants to visit, send a tech, or send a video call (FaceTime/WhatsApp) walk-through
- ✅ Has a real company website that's been online more than a year
- ✅ Sends payment by **wire transfer** without argument
- ✅ Has a rigger's COI ready before they show up
- ✅ Comfortable with as-is, where-is and the standard terms in your bill of sale

## Verification (use these for free)

- **Company website:** does it exist? Look up the domain age at `whois.com` — if it's less than a year old and they claim to be a multinational, scam.
- **Phone:** Google the phone number plus "scam" — known scammers' numbers are in forum posts.
- **Bank:** if they claim to wire from a real bank, that bank can confirm a wire originated. **Your banker — not the buyer — is the one who confirms funds received.** Don't trust email screenshots from the buyer.
- **In doubt?** Call **United Grinding** or one of the Studer specialists in your buyer-call-list and say: "Have you heard of [company name]?" Industry is small. Real buyers are known.

---

## The three-rule version (memorize this)

1. **Wire transfer only. Funds confirmed by my banker. No exceptions.**
2. **Nothing leaves the building until step 1 is done.**
3. **If they pressure me, push past me, or won't answer technical questions — they aren't a buyer.**
